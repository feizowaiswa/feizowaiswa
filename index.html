Lake Mburo National Park

Known for impressive zebra herds and diverse antelope species. Experience unique activities like horseback riding safaris, cycling through savannahs, boat rides on Lake Mburo, and excellent birding opportunities in this accessible park.

East Africa's oldest and largest solitary volcanic mountain, offering spectacular hiking trails, ancient caves, and crater lakes. Experience diverse ecosystems from savannah to alpine zones, with opportunities for cave exploration and wildlife viewing.

Rwenzori National Park

Home to the 'Mountains of the Moon', featuring Africa's third highest peak and equatorial glaciers. Experience breathtaking alpine scenery, diverse wildlife, and challenging hiking trails through this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mountain Hiking Equatorial Glaciers Alpine Flora UNESCO Heritage

Bordering Rwanda and DRC, known for golden monkey tracking and gorilla trekking. Explore volcanic cones, bamboo forests, and scenic crater lakes in this compact, dramatic park.

Essential Packing List for Bwindi Gorilla Trekking

By Baguma Daniel | September 15, 2024

Planning your gorilla trekking adventure in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park? Here's our comprehensive packing guide for a successful encounter with mountain gorillas in Uganda's rainforest. From waterproof gear to sturdy boots, we'll cover everything you need for an unforgettable trekking experience.

Gorilla trekking in Bwindi requires preparation for Uganda's variable weather and dense rainforest terrain. The park's altitude ranges from 1,160 to 2,607 meters, so layering is essential. You'll need waterproof clothing, sturdy hiking boots, and specialized gear for the 2-8 hour treks.

Essential Gear Checklist:

  • Clothing: Waterproof jacket and pants, moisture-wicking base layers, long-sleeved shirts, sturdy hiking pants
  • Footwear: Waterproof hiking boots with good ankle support, moisture-wicking socks
  • Accessories: Waterproof daypack, rain cover, camera protection, insect repellent
  • Health Items: Personal medications, first aid kit, hand sanitizer

What to Expect:

Treks can last 2-8 hours depending on gorilla family location. You'll hike through dense forest, sometimes on steep terrain. The experience of encountering mountain gorillas in their natural habitat is truly unforgettable.

Understanding Ugandan Culture: A Traveler's Guide

By Ajuti Fred | September 10, 2024

Before embarking on your Uganda safari adventure, discover the rich cultural heritage, traditional customs, and warm hospitality that make Uganda's communities so special. From the welcoming "Karibu" greeting to traditional ceremonies, understanding local culture enhances your safari experience.

Uganda is home to over 50 different ethnic groups, each with unique traditions, languages, and customs. The Baganda people, who inhabit the central region including Kampala, have a rich history of kingship and traditional ceremonies. Learning basic Luganda phrases and understanding local etiquette will enrich your interactions with local communities.

Key Cultural Insights:

  • Greetings: "Oli otya?" (How are you?), "Bulungi" (I'm fine), "Webale" (Thank you)
  • Respect: Always greet elders first, use both hands when giving/receiving items
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing is appreciated, especially when visiting cultural sites
  • Photography: Always ask permission before taking photos of people

Cultural Experiences:

Visit the Kabaka's Palace in Kampala, attend traditional ceremonies, and explore local markets to fully immerse yourself in Ugandan culture. The warmth and hospitality of Ugandan people will make your safari experience truly memorable.

Photography Tips for Uganda Wildlife Safari

By Nimusiima Joan | September 5, 2024

Capture the incredible wildlife of Uganda's national parks with these professional photography tips. From golden hour shots at Murchison Falls to intimate portraits of gorillas, learn how to photograph Uganda's diverse wildlife like a pro.

Uganda offers unparalleled wildlife photography opportunities with the Big Five, primates, and unique species found nowhere else. Early morning and late afternoon provide the best light for dramatic shots. Use telephoto lenses for wildlife and wide-angle for landscapes. Remember to respect wildlife safety guidelines while capturing stunning images.

Camera Equipment Recommendations:

  • Lenses: 70-200mm or 100-400mm for wildlife, 16-35mm for landscapes
  • Camera Body: DSLR or mirrorless with good low-light performance
  • Accessories: Extra batteries, memory cards, lens cleaning kit, bean bag for stability
  • Protection: Rain covers, dust protection, camera bag with padding

Photography Tips:

Shoot in RAW format for better post-processing, use burst mode for action shots, and always maintain a safe distance from wildlife. The golden hours (6-8 AM and 5-7 PM) offer the best lighting conditions for dramatic wildlife photography in Uganda's national parks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in the tour price?

Our tour prices include accommodation, meals, transportation, professional guides, permits, and most activities. Flights and personal expenses are not included.

What is your cancellation policy?

Our cancellation policy follows industry standards: 61+ days before departure: 25% cancellation fee; 46-60 days: 50% fee; 31-45 days: 75% fee; 0-30 days: 100% fee. Gorilla permits have separate terms due to UWA regulations. We recommend travel insurance for maximum protection.

What safety measures do you have in place?

All our guides are certified in first aid and emergency response. We carry satellite communication devices and have comprehensive emergency protocols in place.

What fitness level is required?

Our tours range from easy to challenging. We provide detailed fitness requirements for each tour and can recommend training programs to prepare for your adventure.

How do I book a tour?

You can book directly through our website, call our office, or email us. We require a 25% deposit to secure your spot, with the balance due 30 days before departure.

What is the best time to visit Uganda for gorilla trekking?

The best time for gorilla trekking is during Uganda's dry seasons: December to February (peak season) and June to September (shoulder season). These months offer better trekking conditions with less rain and clearer views of the gorillas.

Do I need a gorilla permit and how much does it cost?

Yes, a gorilla permit is required and costs $800 USD per person. Permits are limited to 8 people per gorilla family per day. We include this in our tour packages, but it must be booked in advance as permits sell out quickly.

Do I need a visa to visit Uganda?

Most visitors need a visa for Uganda. You can apply for an e-visa online (recommended) or get one at the airport/border. East African citizens can travel visa-free. We can provide visa guidance and assistance.
