Queen Elizabeth National Park

Famous for tree-climbing lions in the Ishasha sector. Experience thrilling boat cruises on the Kazinga Channel, diverse ecosystems, and abundant wildlife including elephants, hippos, buffaloes, and over 600 bird species.

Tree-Climbing Lions Kazinga Channel Cruises 600+ Bird Species Diverse Wildlife

Murchison Falls National Park

Home to the Nile's most powerful waterfall where the river thunders through a narrow gorge. Enjoy boat cruises on the Nile, look for the shoebill, and encounter big game including elephants, giraffes, and lions.

Murchison Falls Nile River Cruises Shoebill Stork Large Game Safari

Kibale National Park

Uganda's primate capital, famous for chimpanzee trekking and 13 primate species. Explore lush rainforest trails and excellent birding in one of East Africa's richest forest ecosystems.

Chimpanzee Trekking 13 Primate Species Bird Watching Bigodi Wetlands

Kidepo Valley National Park

Known for remote wilderness and authentic African safari experience. Features unique wildlife not common elsewhere in Uganda including cheetahs and ostriches, set against stunning rugged landscapes with opportunities for Karamojong cultural tours.

Remote Wilderness Cheetahs & Ostriches Rugged Landscapes Karamojong Culture

Pian Upe Wildlife Reserve

Among Uganda's largest reserves, famed for sweeping savannahs and rare species. Look for cheetah, ostrich, roan antelope, and exceptional birdlife across the dramatic Karamoja plains.

Vast Savannah Cheetah & Ostrich Roan Antelope Birding Paradise

Lake Mburo National Park

Known for impressive zebra herds and diverse antelope species. Experience unique activities like horseback riding safaris, cycling through savannahs, boat rides on Lake Mburo, and excellent birding opportunities in this accessible park.

Zebra Herds Antelope Species Horseback Riding Cycling Safaris

Mount Elgon National Park

East Africa's oldest and largest solitary volcanic mountain, offering spectacular hiking trails, ancient caves, and crater lakes. Experience diverse ecosystems from savannah to alpine zones, with opportunities for cave exploration and wildlife viewing.

Mountain Hiking Cave Exploration Crater Lakes Diverse Ecosystems

Rwenzori National Park

Home to the 'Mountains of the Moon', featuring Africa's third highest peak and equatorial glaciers. Experience breathtaking alpine scenery, diverse wildlife, and challenging hiking trails through this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mountain Hiking Equatorial Glaciers Alpine Flora UNESCO Heritage

Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

Bordering Rwanda and DRC, known for golden monkey tracking and gorilla trekking. Explore volcanic cones, bamboo forests, and scenic crater lakes in this compact, dramatic park.

Golden Monkey Tracking Mountain Gorilla Trekking Volcanic Landscapes Crater Lakes
